Psalm 79-A

1.God, the nations have invaded

and attacked your heritage;

they defiled your holy temple,

crushed Jerusalem in rage.

They have giv’n your servants’ bodies

to the birds of prey as meat,

and the corpses of your faithful

to wild animals to eat.


2.They have poured out blood like water

all around Jerusalem.

There was no one there to bury

all the bodies of the dead.

We’ve become to all our neighbors

a derision and reproach.

We are scorned by those who mock us,

those who now on us encroach.


3.LORD, how long? Will your displeasure

carry on and never tire?

Will your jealous anger burn, LORD,

like an all-consuming fire?

Pour your fury on the nations

that reject your holy name,

all the kingdoms which devour and

shatter Jacob’s dwelling place.


4.Lord, remember not against us

all our sins of long ago;

may your mercy quickly meet us—

you have brought us very low.

Help us, God of our salvation,

for the glory of your name.

For your name’s sake come and save us,

cover all our sin and shame.


5.Why should nations all around us

come and say, “Where is your God?”

Let the nations know your vengeance

for your servants’ outpoured blood.

May the groans of ev’rypris’ner

come before your throne on high;

and according to your power

save all those condemned to die.


6.Lord, reward our taunting neighbors,

for they treated you with scorn;

in their laps, O Lord, deliver

sev’nfold judgment in return.

Then shall we, your sheep, your people,

give you thanks forevermore,

and thro’ ev’ry generation

your unending praise record.
